Wagering Requirements: What They Mean

Wagering requirements (also called playthrough or rollover) are the number of times you must bet the bonus (and sometimes the deposit) before you can withdraw winnings. For example, 30x on a $100 bonus means you must place $3,000 in total bets. Lower multipliers (e.g. 25x–35x) are generally better. Some casinos apply requirements to bonus only; others to bonus + deposit. Game weighting matters: slots often count 100%, while live casino table games may count 10% or be excluded. Our slot RTP guide helps you choose games that contribute efficiently.

Meeting wagering requirements takes time and bankroll. A 35x requirement on a $200 bonus means $7,000 in bets—at $1 per spin, that's 7,000 spins. High-RTP games reduce expected loss during this process. Avoid high-volatility slots when clearing bonuses unless you have a large bankroll. Some players prefer to skip bonuses entirely and play with real money only; that's valid if you value simplicity over extra funds. Always check whether the requirement applies to bonus only or bonus plus deposit—the latter is significantly harder to complete.
