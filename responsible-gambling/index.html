Signs of Problem Gambling

Recognizing the signs of problem gambling is the first step to getting help. Warning signs include:

  • Spending more money than you can afford to lose
  • Gambling to escape problems or relieve stress
  • Chasing losses by betting more to win back money
  • Lying to family or friends about gambling habits
  • Borrowing money or selling possessions to gamble
  • Neglecting work, family, or other responsibilities
  • Feeling restless or irritable when trying to stop gambling
  • Gambling until all your money is gone

Tips for Responsible Gambling

  1. Set a budget: Decide how much you can afford to lose before you start playing and stick to it.
  2. Set time limits: Decide how long you want to play and stop when your time is up.
  3. Never chase losses: Accept that losing is part of gambling. Don't try to win back what you've lost.
  4. Don't gamble when emotional: Avoid gambling when you're upset, stressed, or depressed.
  5. Take regular breaks: Step away from gambling regularly to maintain perspective.
  6. Balance gambling with other activities: Don't let gambling become your only hobby.
  7. Never borrow money to gamble: Only gamble with money you can afford to lose.
  8. Know the odds: Understand that the house always has an edge.
